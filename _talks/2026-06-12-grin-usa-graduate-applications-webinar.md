I was invited by the **Global Research Innovation Network (GRIN)** to deliver a webinar on **Crafting Strong USA Graduate Applications**, sharing practical strategies for CS and STEM students looking to pursue higher education in the United States.

The session focused on helping students build competitive applications that stand out in a highly selective admissions process.

**Key topics covered:**
- What top universities evaluate beyond GPA. Research experience, projects, publications, leadership, and impact.
- Structuring a strong academic CV/resume to effectively highlights technical skills and achievements.
- Common application mistakes that reduce admission chances, and how to avoid them.
- Presenting achievements strategically by measurable impact rather than simply listing tasks.
- Building compelling Statements of Purpose (SOPs) and Letters of Recommendation (LORs).

The central message of the session: **strong graduate applications are built through clarity, impact, preparation, and authentic storytelling.**
